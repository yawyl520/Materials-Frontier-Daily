## [Fast operando spectro-ptychography reveals battery degradation pathways](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25175) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This work develops a fast operando soft X-ray spectro-ptychography platform that provides nanoscale chemical-state-resolved movies of redox-active electrodes over full battery lifetime, applied to an alkaline Fe anode to reveal reversible charge storage and competing degradation processes. This methodological breakthrough overcomes critical trade-offs among chemical sensitivity, spatial resolution, and temporal resolution, enabling direct nanoscale visualization of charge-storage and degradation pathways. It has high impact for battery research and other electrochemical materials. The method separates fast reversible charge storage via hydroxide insertion from slower dissolution-redeposition that redistributes Fe and enlarges FeOOH particles, ultimately causing capacity loss. It tracks processes at both single-particle and particle ensemble levels.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Operando microscopy techniques often require trade-offs between chemical sensitivity, spatial resolution, and temporal resolution, making it difficult to track nanoscale electrochemical changes over extended timescales. Ptychography is a computational microscopy technique that uses overlapping diffraction patterns to reconstruct high-resolution phase and amplitude images, and when combined with X-ray absorption spectroscopy (spectro-ptychography), it can provide chemical-state information at the nanoscale.

## [Differentiable KKR-CPA Framework Enables Inverse Materials Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25502) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have developed a differentiable DFT framework integrating automatic differentiation into the KKR-CPA method, enabling gradient-based optimization of atomic compositions for inverse materials design. This is demonstrated by discovering candidate magnetic alloys and half-metals from compositional spaces spanning dozens of elements. This work provides a physically grounded, computationally efficient route to discover materials with target properties, overcoming limitations of high-throughput screening and surrogate models. It could accelerate discovery of functional materials in areas such as spintronics and permanent magnets. The method uses reverse-mode automatic differentiation to compute gradients of any computable objective function with respect to composition at a cost independent of the number of candidate elements. Demonstrated applications include optimizing for magnetic moment and half-metallicity, yielding candidates such as (Lu0.553Yb0.447)(Co0.759Fe0.241)2Fe3 and FeZr(Sb0.94Te0.06).

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Inverse materials design aims to find a composition that yields a desired property, but traditional approaches often require many evaluations or suffer from poor extrapolation. The Korringa–Kohn–Rostoker (KKR) method calculates electronic band structure of periodic solids, and the coherent potential approximation (CPA) treats disorder in alloys. Combining them (KKR-CPA) enables efficient electronic structure calculations for random alloys with continuous composition variables.

Multi-harmonic lock-in thermography is a sensitive thermal measurement technique that can detect small temperature oscillations at multiple frequencies.

## [Orbital magnetization key to anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25999) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new study demonstrates that residual orbital magnetization, governed by crystal field and spin-orbit coupling, is intrinsic to the anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets, contradicting prior assumptions that the small remanent magnetization is negligible. This theoretical correction resolves a fundamental puzzle in altermagnet physics and provides a unified framework linking orbital magnetization to anomalous transport, potentially impacting spintronics and the design of magnetic memory devices. The authors use the generalized Středa relation, σ_xy = -e ∂M_z/∂μ, to connect anomalous Hall conductivity with orbital magnetization, and they reveal a microscopic mechanism in MnTe-type altermagnets where magnetic anisotropy generates net orbital moment without Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Altermagnets are a recently discovered class of collinear antiferromagnets with zero net magnetization but non-relativistic spin splitting, making them promising for spintronics. The anomalous Hall effect typically requires net magnetization, yet it has been observed in altermagnets with a small remanent magnetization that was previously considered irrelevant. The Středa relation is a fundamental formula linking Hall conductivity to the derivative of magnetization with respect to chemical potential, providing a thermodynamic connection between transport and magnetization.

## [Quantum Back-Action Expands Excitonic Hilbert Space in Soft Polar Semiconductor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26063) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers show that in lead-halide perovskite nanocrystals, quantum back-action between an electronic excitation and lattice polarization dynamically expands the excitonic Hilbert space, so that excitonic states and their couplings emerge sequentially after photoexcitation, not simultaneously as conventionally assumed. This overturns the conventional hierarchy of electronic excitations in solids, where the excitonic manifold is assumed to be fully formed at the moment of photoexcitation, and has significant implications for understanding quasiparticle formation in soft polar semiconductors like lead-halide perovskites, which are crucial for next-generation optoelectronics. Using femtosecond coherent multidimensional spectroscopy, the study observed that an initial excitonic polarization X1 is followed by the emergence of a second configuration X2 only after the polaron field develops, with coherent X1-X2 coupling appearing at later times; diagonal and anti-diagonal splittings increase with nanocrystal size, opposite to simple quantum confinement expectations.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: In solids, electronic excitations often form excitons—bound electron-hole pairs. Conventionally, the excitonic Hamiltonian is defined first, and lattice vibrations (phonons) only later cause renormalization, relaxation, and dephasing. However, in soft polar semiconductors such as lead-halide perovskites, strong electron-phonon coupling can lead to polaron formation. This work demonstrates that the excitonic Hilbert space—the set of accessible exciton states—is not static but can be actively expanded by quantum back-action from the lattice polarization field.

## [New semilocal functional captures exchange-correlation spin torque](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.04124) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have developed the first family of exchange-correlation functionals within spin-current-density-functional theory (SCDFT) that capture spin-torque effects, and implemented it in VASP. The functional is based on Becke-Roussel exchange and Colle-Salvetti correlation and depends on the spin-current density. This breakthrough addresses a fundamental limitation of semilocal functionals, enabling accurate description of spin dynamics in magnetic materials without resorting to computationally expensive methods like exact exchange. It provides a practical tool for first-principles studies of spintronics and magnetic phenomena. The functional is U(1)×SU(2) gauge-invariant and features a 2×2 exchange-correlation potential. Application to Cr3 and bulk MnO shows spin torque effects of the same order as exact exchange methods, and a counterintuitive energy contribution even in collinear ferromagnets without spin-orbit coupling due to semilocality of magnetization.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Semilocal exchange-correlation functionals, such as LSDA and GGA, lack the exchange-correlation spin torque, limiting their accuracy for non-collinear magnetism and spin dynamics. Spin-current-density-functional theory extends conventional DFT by including paramagnetic currents and spin-current densities as basic variables, ensuring gauge invariance. The new functional incorporates these currents to capture spin-torque effects at semilocal cost.

## [Quantum-Antidot Interferometry Probes Non-Abelian Anyon Braiding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24930) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This paper proposes a novel interferometry scheme using two gate-controlled quantum antidots to directly measure the elementary π-rotation exchange of non-Abelian anyons, enabling a probe of their non-Abelian statistics and topological spins. Directly probing elementary braiding exchanges of non-Abelian anyons is a crucial step toward topological quantum computing, as non-Abelian anyons are the basis for fault-tolerant qubits and this measurement validates their non-Abelian statistics. The scheme distinguishes between single and double cooperative tunnelling processes, allowing measurement of the elementary exchange for unresolved fusion channels, while for resolved fusion channels the double-cooperative process is distinguished by a reduced interference amplitude.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Non-Abelian anyons are quasiparticles in condensed matter systems that obey non-Abelian braiding statistics, meaning exchanging them changes the system's state in a non-commutative way, a key property for topological quantum computing. Conventional Fabry–Perot interferometry only accesses full braids and cannot probe the elementary π-rotation exchange. Quantum antidots are controllable potential barriers in quantum Hall systems that can confine and manipulate anyons, and have been recently proposed for Abelian anyons.

## [Site-controlled InAs/InP quantum dots with record-low fine-structure splitting](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26060) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrated site-controlled growth of InAs/InP quantum dots on truncated InP nanopyramids, achieving fine-structure splitting below 9.2 μeV, the lowest reported for telecom-band quantum dots. This breakthrough enables deterministic entangled-photon sources at telecom wavelengths, critical for scalable quantum communication networks, by overcoming the random positioning and shape anisotropy of conventional self-assembled quantum dots. The quantum dots nucleate at symmetric apexes of truncated nanopyramids via metal-organic vapor-phase epitaxy, suppressing anisotropic growth. Single-photon purity was confirmed with g^(2)(0)=0.07, and emission spans S, C, and L telecom bands with no lithography-induced defects.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Quantum dots (QDs) are nanoscale semiconductor crystals that confine electrons and holes, emitting photons at specific wavelengths. For entangled photon generation, the exciton fine-structure splitting (FSS) must be minimized; typically self-assembled QDs have FSS of tens of μeV. Site-controlled growth uses patterned substrates to precisely position QDs, addressing a key limitation for scalable quantum photonics.

## [KuboMap: Real-Space Mapping of Electronic Conductivity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24914) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A new method called KuboMap provides a real-space representation of electronic conductivity derived from the Kubo-Greenwood formula, enabling direct visualization of transport-active regions in complex materials. This work offers transparent physical insight into how conductivity arises in disordered systems such as amorphous silicon and oxides, which is crucial for designing electronic and optoelectronic materials. KuboMap defines a nonnegative conductivity density whose spatial integral recovers the total conductivity. It was demonstrated on aluminum, amorphous silicon, and silicon oxides, distinguishing metallic, insulating, and hopping-like transport pathways.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: The Kubo-Greenwood formula is a linear response theory that calculates electrical conductivity from electronic structure. Traditional implementations yield a single scalar conductivity value, obscuring spatial details. KuboMap bridges this gap by providing a physically motivated real-space decomposition based on Mott's picture of hopping transport.

The study demonstrates that the molecular-valve effect in RHO zeolites is a generic property of the isoreticular family, with critical pressure tunable from ~0.94 GPa for parent RHO down to a predicted ≤0.03 GPa for PST-28, an order of magnitude reduction. This finding transforms RHO zeolites from a curious single material into a tunable platform for pressure-responsive nanoporous solids, enabling design of soft frameworks for stimuli-responsive applications such as sensing or controlled release. The study combines lattice dynamics, full elastic-constant tensors, and finite-temperature free-energy reconstructions within a classical core-shell model validated against DFT. The critical pressure is a mechanical hydrostatic pressure of the bare pure-silica framework, not a gas adsorption pressure.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 25, 04:00

**Background**: Zeolites are microporous aluminosilicate minerals with well-defined pore structures. The RHO zeolite family exhibits a unique molecular-valve effect: a reversible phase transition between centric and acentric structures triggered by external stimuli like pressure or gas adsorption. The isoreticular family shares the same topology but different unit cell sizes, enabling systematic property tuning.

The study uses ammonium sulfate as a model barocaloric material, comparing training from scratch, naïve fine-tuning, and multihead replay fine-tuning of the MACE-MPA-0 model, finding that fine-tuned models require only 5-10 60-atom DFT configurations, and that dispersion corrections are necessary for correct phase behavior.

Machine-learned interatomic potentials (MLIPs) enable molecular dynamics simulations at near-DFT accuracy, but generating DFT training data is computationally expensive. Fine-tuning pre-trained foundation models like MACE-MPA-0 can drastically reduce data requirements, as shown here for ammonium sulfate.
