## [Arsenene multilayers show thickness-dependent semiconductor-to-semimetal crossover](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19780) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A study combining diffusion quantum Monte Carlo (DMC) and DFT reveals that interlayer coupling in arsenene multilayers depends on thickness and coordination, not just registry, leading to a semiconductor-to-semimetal crossover as layers increase. This challenges the common assumption that bilayer interlayer interactions directly transfer to bulk, providing critical insights for designing 2D materials with tunable electronic properties, especially for arsenene and related layered materials. DMC shows bulk gray arsenic is compact, but few-layer structures remain at larger separations despite the same registry. SCAN+rVV10 best reproduces DMC results, predicting a thickness-driven stacking sequence from A1A1 to A1B1 to bulk-like A1B-1, with band gap collapse due to enhanced interlayer As pz hybridization.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: Arsenene is a 2D material similar to graphene but made of arsenic atoms, with potential applications in electronics and optoelectronics. Interlayer coupling in layered materials often determines their electronic properties; diffusion quantum Monte Carlo (DMC) is a highly accurate method for electronic structure calculations, used as a benchmark for density functional theory (DFT) functionals like SCAN+rVV10, which accounts for van der Waals interactions.

This work provides direct experimental evidence of a previously unknown long-lived bulk photovoltaic mechanism, which cannot be explained by shift or ballistic currents. It opens new pathways for controlling photoresponses via defect engineering, with implications for next-generation photovoltaics and quantum materials. The experiment was performed on single-crystal, monodomain BiFeO3 using contactless pump-probe microscopy with high spatiotemporal resolution. Monte Carlo simulations attribute the nanosecond-scale asymmetric carrier transport to asymmetric momentum scattering from oxygen vacancies, a mechanism distinct from conventional shift or ballistic currents.

Conventional BPVE mechanisms include shift current (intrinsic) and ballistic current (from asymmetric scattering), both typically short-lived (picosecond scale). Pump-probe microscopy is an ultrafast technique that captures carrier dynamics with femtosecond temporal and nanometer spatial resolution.

The on-the-fly approach builds MLFFs adaptively during simulation, using Bayesian inference to decide when to perform expensive AIMD calculations, thereby achieving efficiency without sacrificing accuracy.

## [Three-Dimensional Foliated Fractional Quantum Hall Phases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19426) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This study demonstrates that a stack of fractional quantum Hall layers at filling ν=1/3 can host a stable foliated topological order. It further shows that the system can spontaneously enter a non-Abelian Fibonacci phase, as supported by numerical exact diagonalization of up to 10 layers and perturbative analytical calculations. This work is significant because it establishes the existence of a stable non-Abelian Fibonacci phase in a three-dimensional layered system, which is a promising platform for topological quantum computation. It also provides a realistic route to realizing these phases in layered van der Waals crystals and multilayer heterostructures under strong magnetic fields. The study focuses on filling ν=1/3 of the lowest Landau level in each layer, and uses pseudopotential interactions within and between neighboring layers. The foliated Fibonacci phase is identified via quasihole counting and overlap with a model wave function derived from conformal field theory, specifically in a 9-layer system.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: Fractional quantum Hall (FQH) states are topologically ordered phases that host anyons—quasiparticles with fractional statistics. Foliated topological orders extend this to three dimensions, where anyons are confined to layers. Non-Abelian anyons, such as Fibonacci anyons, are particularly interesting for topological quantum computation because their braiding can perform universal quantum gates. Pseudopotentials are a standard method to model interactions in FQH systems projected to a single Landau level.
