## [Ultrafast non-thermal suppression of ferroelectricity by carrier screening in LiNbO3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27494) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrate that femtosecond laser pulses can transiently suppress ferroelectric polarization in LiNbO3 via carrier screening without heating, as evidenced by time-resolved second-harmonic generation and stimulated Raman scattering measurements. This discovery provides a reversible, ultrafast mechanism for controlling ferroelectric order, which could lead to faster, more energy-efficient electronic devices and new possibilities for quantum materials. Even dilute photoexcited carriers cause rapid and enduring suppression of both polarization and Raman susceptibility. Fluence- and temperature-dependent analyses confirm the effect is non-thermal and symmetry-preserving.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Ferroelectric materials have a spontaneous electric polarization that can be switched by an electric field. Carrier screening occurs when free charges accumulate and partially cancel the internal electric field, which usually suppresses ferroelectricity. In equilibrium, screening is detrimental, but this work shows that transient photoexcited carriers can reversibly modulate ferroelectricity on ultrafast timescales without significant heating.

## [BRAVE: Risk-Aware Bayesian Optimization for High-Entropy Alloys](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27522) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers developed the BRAVE (Bayesian Risk-aware Alloy discoVery and Exploration) framework, which integrates a learned feasibility classifier into multi-objective Bayesian optimization to probabilistically penalize risky candidates while preserving access to high-performing boundary compositions. From approximately 27,000 CALPHAD-screened candidates, they synthesized 48 alloys in three closed-loop iterations, exploring only 0.12% of the feasible space. This work addresses the critical challenge of exploring phase stability boundaries where high-performance compositions reside but risk of failure is high. The risk-aware approach enables efficient discovery of high-entropy alloys with exceptional properties, potentially accelerating materials design in multi-objective optimization problems. The campaign targeted five objectives including yield strength and ductility, identifying two compositional regimes: a V-rich, Ni-rich high-strength regime (UTS up to ~1480 MPa at 50% elongation) and a Mn-containing high-ductility regime (UTS/YS ratio up to 4.20). Vanadium showed dual roles, driving both yield strength (r=0.84) and sigma-phase formation (r=0.54 with infeasibility), consistent with the KKT prediction that constrained optima lie on active constraint boundaries.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: High-entropy alloys (HEAs) are multi-principal-element alloys that can exhibit exceptional mechanical properties, but their vast compositional space makes discovery challenging. Bayesian optimization is a sequential design strategy that models an unknown objective function and selects the most promising candidates to evaluate next. The CALPHAD (CALculation of PHAse Diagrams) method uses thermodynamic databases to predict phase stability, often used as a screening tool. A key insight is that optimal compositions often lie at phase stability boundaries, where experimental failure risk is highest.

## [PtyRANNOSAUR: Neural Network for Ultrafast Electron Ptychography](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27587) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers developed PtyRANNOSAUR, a convolutional autoencoder-based neural network that reconstructs atomic-resolution electron ptychography data 10-100 times faster than standard iterative methods, achieving sub-0.5 Å resolution. This breakthrough enables near-live electron ptychography reconstructions, drastically reducing processing time and making high-resolution imaging more accessible for materials science. PtyRANNOSAUR handles experimental imperfections such as spatial partial coherence, multiple scattering, and scan position errors without hyperparameter tuning. It was tested on both simulated and experimental data across various materials systems, achieving reconstructions comparable to iterative methods.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Electron ptychography is a computational microscopy technique that reconstructs the complex-valued image (amplitude and phase) of a specimen from a series of coherent diffraction patterns recorded as a focused electron beam scans across the sample with overlap. 4D STEM captures a full diffraction pattern at each scan position, generating large datasets that require computationally intensive iterative phase retrieval. PtyRANNOSAUR uses a convolutional autoencoder, a deep learning architecture designed for image processing, to learn a direct mapping from 4D STEM data to phase images, drastically reducing reconstruction time.

## [Dipole-Glass Design Enables Record Cryogenic Energy Storage](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27887) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers achieved superior cryogenic energy storage by constructing a unit-cell-level disordered dipole-glass state in Pb0.6Sr0.4ZrO3 thin films, delivering a record-high energy density of 211 J/cm³ at 9 MV/cm and efficiency over 88% down to 4 K. This breakthrough addresses the fundamental limitation of conventional batteries and dielectric capacitors at cryogenic temperatures, enabling reliable energy storage for deep-space exploration and quantum computing applications. The dipole-glass state is achieved near the antiferroelectric-paraelectric phase boundary, introducing unit-cell-level complexity that suppresses long-range ferroelectric order. The films exhibit ultralow hysteresis, microsecond charge/discharge capability, and stability over 10⁸ cycles.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Cryogenic energy storage is critical for deep-space missions and quantum computers, but conventional batteries fail below ~230 K due to frozen ion migration. Relaxor ferroelectric capacitors offer high efficiency at room temperature but suffer from increased hysteresis at cryogenic temperatures due to freezing of polar nanodomains. A dipole glass is a disordered state where electric dipoles are frozen in random orientations, eliminating long-range order and resulting in ultralow hysteresis. This work uses an antiferroelectric-derived dipole glass to achieve both high energy density and efficiency at 4 K.

## [Multiphoton Absorption Fingerprints Altermagnetic Spin Splittings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27548) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This theoretical work shows that multiphoton absorption, when resolved by polarization, can directly fingerprint the angular harmonic of altermagnetic spin splittings. Specifically, two-, four-, and six-photon absorption correspond to d-, g-, and i-wave orders, respectively, providing a clear hierarchy beyond linear response. This work establishes a direct nonlinear optical probe for altermagnetism, a newly discovered quantum state of matter with potential for spintronics. The proposed fingerprints enable experimental distinction of different altermagnetic orders, which is crucial for advancing condensed matter physics and materials science. The direct n-photon contribution to the transition matrix element is absent in a specific polarization channel locked to the symmetry harmonic of the altermagnetic texture. This absence alters the frequency scaling of the absorption rate, providing a clear optical fingerprint.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Altermagnetism is a recently identified magnetic phase with zero net magnetization but spin-split electronic bands due to crystal symmetry, distinct from conventional antiferromagnets. Multiphoton absorption is a nonlinear optical process where two or more photons are absorbed simultaneously to excite a material. This work combines these concepts to propose a polarization-resolved spectroscopy technique for altermagnetic materials.

## [LitMOF: LLM Multi-Agent Corrects MOF Database Errors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.01693) ⭐️ 9.0/10

LitMOF uses a multi-agent LLM framework to validate and correct structural errors in metal-organic framework (MOF) databases, producing a curated dataset of 189,567 computation-ready structures and repairing 9,227 invalid entries. Structural errors in MOF databases severely distort high-throughput screening and machine learning predictions, limiting data-driven discovery. LitMOF provides a scalable, generalizable approach for self-correcting scientific databases, significantly improving the reliability of MOF research. Applied to the CSD MOF Subset, LitMOF successfully repaired 69.1% of the not-computation-ready MOFs in the current CoRE MOF DB and uncovered 8,771 experimentally reported MOFs absent from existing databases. A case study on direct air capture screening showed that uncorrected errors lead to systematic misranking of materials, false positives, and omission of high-performance candidates.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) are porous crystalline materials widely studied for gas storage, separation, and catalysis. Large MOF databases like the CSD MOF Subset and CoRE MOF DB are used for high-throughput screening and machine learning, but nearly half of their entries contain structural errors due to inconsistencies in literature extraction. Correcting these errors traditionally requires manual expert validation, which is time-consuming and not scalable.

## [Topological Charge Splits in Non-Hermitian Skyrmions at Exceptional Points](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27810) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new theoretical study reveals that in non-Hermitian magnetic skyrmions, topological protection splits into two distinct charges, and the biorthogonal charge breaks down at an exceptional point ring on the skyrmion's equator. This work fundamentally changes the understanding of topological protection in non-Hermitian systems, showing that it is not a single invariant but can be fragmented. It has broad implications for topological physics, spintronics, and non-Hermitian photonics. The paper introduces two topological charges: a homotopy-protected charge from the right eigenstate and a complex biorthogonal charge from the left-right pair. The biorthogonal charge loses quantization and diverges at an exceptional point ring, which is the real-space analog of analyticity breakdown in causal response functions.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Magnetic skyrmions are topologically protected spin textures with integer topological charge, which prevents them from unwinding. Non-Hermitian physics describes open systems with gain and loss, where exceptional points are singularities where eigenvalues and eigenvectors coalesce. This paper bridges these concepts by studying skyrmions in a PT-symmetric non-Hermitian magnet.

## [Eliminating Flux Trapping in Superconducting Circuits Using Thermal Gradients](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27415) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have developed a practical method that uses on-chip resistive heaters to create local thermal gradients, driving magnetic vortices into moats and eliminating flux trapping during cooldown in ambient magnetic fields up to 60 μT. This breakthrough overcomes a long-standing challenge in superconducting electronics and quantum computing, where flux trapping degrades device performance, and paves the way for scalable high-performance superconducting circuits without complex magnetic shielding. The method was validated using magnetic imaging and electrical readout, showing it can remove flux trapped during field cooling as well as flux nucleated by circuit operation. With basic magnetic shielding, it can suppress all magnetic flux in large-scale circuits.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Superconducting circuits can trap magnetic vortices when cooled in ambient magnetic fields, causing performance degradation. Moats (etched holes) are commonly used to sequester flux, but vortices can still become pinned in active regions. Thermal gradients exert a force on vortices, moving them toward hot regions, enabling controlled manipulation.
