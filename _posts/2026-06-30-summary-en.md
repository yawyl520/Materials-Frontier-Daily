## [Industry-ready spin-photon interfaces for quantum computing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27787) ⭐️ 10.0/10

Researchers demonstrated thousands of industrially fabricated quantum-dot spin-photon interfaces on a III-V pilot production line, achieving near-unity photon purity, record Wigner-function negativity, and seven-partite spin-multi-photon entanglement. This breakthrough shows that hybrid photonic quantum computing can be scaled using foundry-compatible processes, directly addressing key challenges in error correction and large-scale deployment by providing reproducible, high-performance devices. The devices achieve a path to optical losses below fault-tolerance thresholds, with stable near-unity quantum purity over tens of minutes and spin coherence extending to microsecond timescales in low magnetic fields. Photons from distant sources are as indistinguishable as photons from a single source.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Hybrid photonic quantum computers combine stationary matter qubits (e.g., quantum dots) with flying photonic qubits to enable networked quantum computation. A key component is the spin-photon interface, which efficiently generates indistinguishable single photons and creates spin-photon entanglement. Achieving this with high yield and reproducibility is essential for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

## [PtyRANNOSAUR: Neural network for ultrafast electron ptychography](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27587) ⭐️ 9.0/10

PtyRANNOSAUR is a convolutional autoencoder-based code that reconstructs atomic-resolution electron ptychography data 10-100x faster than iterative methods, handling spatial partial coherence, multiple scattering, and scan errors. This breakthrough enables near-live, state-of-the-art electron ptychography reconstructions, drastically accelerating materials characterization at the atomic scale and making advanced imaging more accessible. The neural networks achieve resolution below 0.5 Å, comparable to the best iterative reconstructions, and are pre-trained on a large database of crystal structures, requiring no hyperparameter tuning for new datasets.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Electron ptychography is a computational microscopy technique that reconstructs the complex-valued image of a specimen from overlapping diffraction patterns, providing aberration-free, high-resolution phase images. 4D scanning transmission electron microscopy (4D STEM) records a diffraction pattern at each scan position, generating a 4D dataset. Traditional iterative reconstruction algorithms are computationally intensive, taking minutes to hours, which limits real-time applications.

## [Al/Ge Heterostructure with Atomically Sharp Commensurate Interface](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27854) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers successfully grew single-crystalline Al(111) on Ge(111) using molecular beam epitaxy, achieving an atomically sharp interface with a commensurate 7-Al-lattice to 5-Ge-lattice relationship that reduces intrinsic lattice mismatch from 28.4% to about 0.1%. This breakthrough overcomes the critical issue of Al-Ge interdiffusion that degraded quantum device performance. The atomically flat and sharp interface provides a promising platform for studying topological quantum states and developing high-quality quantum electronic devices. The ordered interface only forms above a critical thickness of 0.3 nm; below that, Al nucleates as random clusters that transform into 2D islands and eventually a continuous film. Optimized growth yields an ultra-flat film with surface roughness of 0.16 nm and an ultra-thin continuous film as thin as 2 nm.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Al/Ge heterostructures are of interest for quantum devices such as topological qubits, but interdiffusion at the interface—especially through grain boundaries in polycrystalline films—degrades device performance. Molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) is a technique that deposits thin films with atomic precision, but epitaxial growth typically requires good lattice matching; a large lattice mismatch often leads to defects. This work demonstrates that a commensurate 7:5 epitaxial relationship can effectively accommodate the large mismatch, enabling single-crystalline growth.

## [Dipole Glass Enables Record Cryogenic Energy Storage in Thin Films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27887) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have demonstrated a dipole-glass state in Pb0.6Sr0.4ZrO3 thin films that achieves a record energy density of 211 J/cm^3 with >88% efficiency at 4 K, enabling cryogenic energy storage for deep-space and quantum computing applications. This breakthrough overcomes the fundamental limitation of conventional dielectric capacitors, which suffer from frozen ion migration below ~230 K, by using a unit-cell-level disordered dipole-glass design that suppresses long-range ferroelectric order and hysteresis losses. The dipole-glass state is achieved by compositional tuning near the antiferroelectric-paraelectric phase boundary in Pb0.6Sr0.4ZrO3, introducing enhanced unit-cell-level dipole interaction complexity. The films also demonstrate stability over 10^8 charge/discharge cycles and microsecond-scale charge/discharge capability.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: A dipole glass is a disordered system where electric dipoles are frozen in random orientations below a freezing temperature, similar to spin glasses in magnetism. This randomness prevents long-range ferroelectric order, reducing hysteresis. Conventional relaxor ferroelectrics rely on polar nanodomains that freeze at cryogenic temperatures, causing energy losses. The new approach reduces polar order to the unit-cell level, maintaining low hysteresis even at 4 K.

## [Hidden valley dynamics explain vanishing circular polarization in moiré excitons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27963) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This study resolves the puzzle of near-zero steady-state valley polarization in moiré excitons by revealing a temporal crossing between co- and cross-circularly polarized emission components, using helicity-resolved time-resolved photoluminescence. A minimal two-channel model captures the observed dynamics without invoking fast valley relaxation. This finding corrects a common misinterpretation in valleytronics, showing that time-integrated circular polarization can give false-negative indications of valley polarization. It also demonstrates electrical control of hidden valley dynamics, which is crucial for future valleytronic and quantum-optical devices based on moiré excitons. The key experimental technique is helicity-resolved time-resolved photoluminescence, which reveals a temporal crossing of emission from A-like and B-like moiré channels with opposite optical selection rules and distinct decay/depolarization rates. Two-dimensional gate-field maps show the crossing time evolves systematically with electrostatic tuning.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Moiré excitons in transition-metal dichalcogenide heterostructures have valley degrees of freedom that can be optically addressed, making them promising for valleytronics. Conventional time-integrated measurements of circular polarization often yield near-zero values, which has been interpreted as fast valley relaxation. This study shows that such vanishing polarization can instead arise from the compensation of two oppositely polarized emission channels with different dynamics.

## [Classical wave localization distinct from electronic Anderson model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28262) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This paper rigorously shows that scalar classical waves with mass or force-constant disorder form a distinct constrained disorder class governed by the acoustic sum rule, not directly mappable to standard electronic Anderson models. Phase diagrams reveal that classical-wave systems only localize near band edges, unlike diagonal-disorder electronic systems. This corrects a fundamental misconception in the Anderson localization field, providing a unified perspective that impacts the design of photonic and acoustic media. It clarifies the conditions for Anderson localization in three-dimensional classical-wave systems. The study compares mass and force-constant disorder in classical waves to diagonal and off-diagonal disorder in electronic tight-binding models using spectral and eigenvector diagnostics. The acoustic sum rule correlates diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements, producing localization topologies that differ fundamentally from both diagonal- and off-diagonal-disorder electronic systems.

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Anderson localization describes the absence of wave diffusion in disordered systems, originally for electrons in a crystal. Classical waves such as sound and light can also localize, and researchers often map classical-wave equations to the electronic Anderson model with diagonal disorder. This paper shows that mapping is invalid because classical waves obey the acoustic sum rule, which imposes a constraint that changes the localization behavior.

## [Quantum Semiconductor Heterostructures for meV Axion Dark Matter Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.14320) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new class of detectors called SQWAREs (Semiconductor-Quantum-Well Axion Radiometer Experiments) has been proposed, using quantum semiconductor heterostructures to resonantly enhance axion-photon conversion via the inverse Primakoff effect for meV mass axion dark matter detection. This proposal could close a critical sensitivity gap in direct axion dark matter searches in the meV mass range, potentially probing the well-motivated QCD axion parameter space and representing a paradigm shift in dark matter detection. SQWAREs employ multiple quantum well structures forming magnetoplasmonic cavities with high-mobility 2D electron gases, realizing tunable epsilon-near-zero resonances in the terahertz frequency range; the resonance frequency and axion-induced current are optimized in situ by controlling the cavity orientation in a strong magnetic field.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: The inverse Primakoff effect is the conversion of an axion into a photon in the presence of a strong electromagnetic field, which underpins many axion detection schemes. Magnetoplasmonic cavities combine plasmonic and magnetic properties to enhance light-matter interactions. Epsilon-near-zero resonances occur when the permittivity of a material approaches zero, enabling strong field confinement and enhanced nonlinear effects. SQWAREs leverage these concepts to achieve resonant axion-photon conversion at terahertz frequencies.

## [Risk-aware Bayesian optimization for high-entropy alloy discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27522) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A risk-aware Bayesian optimization campaign, termed BRAVE, integrated a learned feasibility classifier into the multi-objective acquisition function to explore high-entropy alloy compositions near phase stability boundaries, synthesizing 48 alloys over three closed-loop iterations targeting five mechanical objectives. This approach addresses the critical challenge of optimizing alloy compositions at phase boundaries where high performance often accompanies high experimental risk, offering a more efficient and successful strategy for materials discovery. The campaign explored 0.12% of the feasible space from ~27,000 CALPHAD-screened candidates, revealing two compositional regimes: a V-rich, Ni-rich high-strength regime (UTS up to ~1480 MPa at 50% elongation) and a Mn-containing high-ductility regime (UTS/YS up to 4.20 at >50% elongation).

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: High-entropy alloys (HEAs) are multi-principal-element alloys that can exhibit exceptional mechanical properties. CALPHAD (Calculation of Phase Diagrams) is a computational method used to predict phase stability in multicomponent systems. Bayesian optimization is a machine-learning technique for efficient global optimization of expensive black-box functions, often used in materials design. The key innovation here is integrating a risk-aware classifier to handle the trade-off between high performance and experimental failure near stability boundaries.

## [Entropy engineering boosts energy density in lead-free ceramics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27621) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers achieved a record recoverable energy density of 10.55 J/cm³ and breakdown strength of 840 kV/cm in lead-free BF-BT-based high-entropy ceramics through multicomponent B-site substitution. This work demonstrates entropy engineering as a practical route to overcome the trade-off between breakdown strength and polarization in dielectric capacitors, advancing lead-free energy storage materials. The high-entropy composition BaTi0.2Zr0.2Sn0.2Hf0.2Nb0.1Sc0.1O3 was introduced into the BF-BT matrix, inducing lattice distortion, relaxor behavior, and grain refinement that suppress hysteresis and enhance breakdown strength.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 29, 04:00

**Background**: Dielectric capacitors store electrical energy via polarization but are limited by low breakdown strength and hysteresis in lead-free ceramics. High-entropy ceramics incorporate multiple elements in equal or near-equal proportions to stabilize disordered structures, often enhancing properties. Relaxor ferroelectrics exhibit diffuse phase transitions and slim hysteresis loops, beneficial for energy storage.
