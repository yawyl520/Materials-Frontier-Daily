## [Room-Temperature Noncollinear Ferroelectricity in WO2Cl2 Demonstrated](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21149) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers experimentally demonstrated room-temperature ferroelectricity in van der Waals WO2Cl2, achieving a wide bandgap of 2.80 eV and a large dipole of ~3 eÅ, with direct atomic-scale observation of noncollinear dipole order. This breakthrough overcomes the key limitations of low-dimensional ferroelectrics—small bandgaps and faint polarization—by combining a wide bandgap with large polarization, opening new routes for nanoelectronics and novel polar physics. Multiple characterizations (second harmonic generation, piezoresponse force microscopy, ferroelectric hysteresis) confirmed ferroelectricity, and integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy directly resolved the noncollinear dipole order at atomic scale. The material follows the d0 rule from ferroelectric perovskites, with off-center W6+ displacement.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Low-dimensional ferroelectrics, such as 2D materials, are promising for nanoelectronics but often suffer from small bandgaps (<2 eV) or weak polarization (<1 μC/cm²). The d0 rule, inherited from perovskite ferroelectrics, requires the cation to have an empty d-shell (d0) to allow off-center displacement and ferroelectricity. Noncollinear dipole order, where dipoles are not aligned in a simple parallel or antiparallel manner, is rare but was predicted in dioxydihalides like WO2Cl2.

## [Spin-orbit coupling induces Fermi-surface splitting in noncollinear antiferromagnet SmBi](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22049) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrate that spin-orbit coupling (SOC) alone can induce Fermi-surface splitting in the compensated noncollinear antiferromagnet SmBi, revealed through quantum oscillations, symmetry analysis, and first-principles calculations. This work establishes a new relativistic mechanism for spin splitting in antiferromagnets, expanding the scope of antiferromagnetic spintronics beyond systems that rely solely on nonrelativistic magnetic order. New quantum oscillation branches appear below the Néel temperature TN and further reconstruct below T*, while isostructural SmSb shows no comparable change. The splitting arises only from the cooperative interplay of noncollinear magnetic order and SOC, which locks spins to the lattice and breaks residual spin-space symmetries.

rss · arXiv Strongly Correlated Electrons · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Noncollinear antiferromagnets have magnetic moments that are not aligned parallel or antiparallel but arranged in a more complex pattern, while exhibiting negligible net magnetization. Fermi-surface splitting refers to the lifting of spin degeneracy in the electronic band structure, which is key for spintronic applications. Previously, such splitting in compensated antiferromagnets was thought to require nonrelativistic symmetry breaking without SOC.

## [Reversible magnon spin transport in ferroelastic altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22186) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This paper proposes a universal mechanism for reversible, nonrelativistic magnon spin transport control in two-dimensional altermagnets using ferroelastic transitions, allowing sign reversal of spin Seebeck and spin Nernst conductivities without external magnetic fields. This work addresses a long-standing challenge in spintronics by enabling nonvolatile, field-free control of magnon spin currents. It opens new pathways for low-dissipation, reconfigurable antiferromagnetic magnonic devices. The mechanism relies on magnetoelastic coupling that reorients crystal axes and modulates magnetic exchange anisotropy without requiring Berry curvature or external fields. It is validated using first-principles calculations and spin-model analyses for a CoTe2 monolayer.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Altermagnets are a recently discovered class of materials that exhibit zero net magnetization like antiferromagnets but have spin-split electronic bands, enabling spintronic functionality. Ferroelastic materials can be switched between different orientational states by applying stress. This work integrates these two phenomena to control magnon spin transport.

## [Coherent seeding and control of dynamical ferroelectricity via phonon anharmonicity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22896) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrate that intense terahertz excitation of a soft phonon mode induces a ferroelectric state in centrosymmetric PbTe, and achieve coherent amplification and suppression of the induced polarization using a double-pulse protocol. This work unifies two major directions in optical control of quantum materials—creating non-equilibrium states and coherently controlling dynamics—and establishes terahertz-driven anharmonic mode coupling as a general strategy for ultrafast information processing. The light-induced ferroelectric state persists up to about 100 K and does not rely on local dipolar fluctuations, unlike previous demonstrations in quantum paraelectrics. The mechanism involves anharmonic coupling between degenerate transverse optical phonons under terahertz driving.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Ferroelectric materials exhibit spontaneous electric polarization that can be switched by an electric field, but most are polar at equilibrium. Centrosymmetric materials like PbTe lack inversion symmetry breaking, so ferroelectricity is not expected. Soft phonon modes are lattice vibrations that can drive structural phase transitions when their frequency approaches zero. Terahertz excitation can resonantly drive these modes to induce transient symmetry breaking.

## [Atomistic Language Models Unify Text and Crystal Structures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21395) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers introduce Atomistic Language Models (ALMs), which seamlessly integrate a pretrained atomistic encoder, a large language model, and a denoising diffusion model via continuous projectors and staged training, achieving state-of-the-art results in crystal structure prediction and de novo generation. This unification of natural language and atomistic structure modeling could dramatically accelerate crystal design and discovery, enabling researchers to generate and optimize materials directly from textual instructions without requiring separate specialized models. ALMs employ a continuous bridge that maps language model embeddings into the steering space of atomistic diffusion, and are assisted by a Text-to-Crystal Feynman-Kac (T2C-FK) sampler that enforces stoichiometric constraints during inference; the team also releases ALM Bench, the first benchmark for text-conditioned crystal generation and optimization.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Crystal structure prediction (CSP) aims to find the most stable arrangement of atoms in a periodic lattice, which is crucial for materials discovery. Traditional methods rely on computationally expensive first-principles calculations, while recent machine learning approaches use diffusion models to generate crystal geometries. Atomistic language models extend this by incorporating natural language understanding, allowing researchers to describe desired material properties in text.

## [Solid-state transcapacitor: new logic, memory, interconnect element](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21772) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers propose a solid-state transcapacitor (TCAP), a three-terminal logic element that uses displacement current instead of conductive current, potentially overcoming the Boltzmann limit and energy scaling barriers of transistors. This innovation could dramatically reduce energy consumption in logic and memory for AI systems, where interconnect energy dominates, and offers a path 100-fold lower energy use with delays comparable to scaled CMOS. The TCAP employs a piezoelectric transcapacitor where a gate-controlled stressor modulates channel capacitance via electromechanical coupling, achieving inversion and gain, and is functionally equivalent to a 1T-1C memory cell for dense memory.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Transistors, the building blocks of modern electronics, face energy scaling challenges due to the Boltzmann limit on subthreshold swing and the need for high current density. The solid-state transcapacitor replaces dissipative transport current with displacement current, which is not bound by this limit and can operate at lower voltages. Displacement current arises from changing electric fields in dielectrics, distinct from conduction current in conductors.

## [Oxygen, not boron threshold, controls hBN pore shape](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.13180) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This study shows that oxygen presence, not the boron displacement threshold energy, determines whether electron-irradiated hexagonal boron nitride forms circular or triangular pores. Resolving a nearly two-decade-old puzzle, this finding enables deterministic creation of atomically defined pores in hBN, which is critical for applications in membranes, quantum technologies, and defect engineering in 2D materials. Experiments in ultra-high vacuum produce circular pores, while even small amounts of oxygen lead to triangular pores with nitrogen-terminated edges. The effect is independent of sample type, manufacturer, electron energy, and beam mode.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) is a 2D material similar to graphene. Under electron irradiation, it was known to form triangular pores, attributed to the lower displacement threshold energy of boron atoms. Displacement threshold energy is the minimum kinetic energy needed to permanently displace an atom from its lattice site. This study challenges that assumption by showing oxygen's chemical role.

## [Ab initio framework for electronic-order-induced lattice symmetry breaking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.09253) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A general ab initio framework based on molecular Berry curvature (MBC) theory has been developed to capture electronic-order-driven symmetry breaking in lattice dynamics, validated on Co3Sn2S2 by reproducing experimental phonon splittings. This work overcomes a long-standing limitation of conventional ab initio methods that cannot describe phonon time-reversal symmetry breaking, enabling predictive discovery of materials with phonon magnetism and Hall-type lattice responses. The framework distinguishes two microscopic origins: Eg mode splitting is governed by MBC, while Eu mode splitting is enhanced by Fano resonance. Using this algorithm, several candidate materials for nonzero symmetry breaking are predicted.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Conventional ab initio lattice dynamics relies on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and cannot capture time-reversal symmetry breaking. Molecular Berry curvature (MBC) is a geometric phase accumulated by electronic states during atomic motion, analogous to Berry curvature in electronic bands. Fano resonance describes asymmetric phonon lineshapes arising from interference between discrete phonons and continuous electron-hole excitations. This study integrates MBC and Fano corrections to achieve accurate phonon spectra.

A new study introduces an open quantum cellular automaton model showing that quantum coherence can drive particle-size segregation beyond classical diffusive limits, producing more strongly segregated steady states. This result challenges the classical understanding of segregation in granular materials and suggests that quantum effects could be harnessed to control transport in dissipative many-body systems, with potential implications for materials science and quantum thermodynamics. The model combines coherent transport and dissipative segregation, reproducing experimental segregation dynamics and showing that weakly decohering systems achieve higher segregation than classical predictions, with steady-state behavior collapsed onto two dimensionless numbers.

Quantum coherence, which typically decays via decoherence, is here shown to enhance segregation rather than being suppressed. This work extends the concept of quantum cellular automata—a model of quantum computation with locality—to open quantum systems.

## [Continuous Hyperelastic Manifolds Exhibit Higher-Order Topological Phases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22710) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrated that continuous, homogeneous hyperelastic manifolds under finite multiaxial deformations can host higher-order topological phases, including 1D hinge states and 0D zero-energy corner states, without requiring discrete metamaterial structures. This work shifts the paradigm of mechanical topological insulators from engineered discrete lattices to intrinsic continuous materials, opening the door for programmable soft matter devices with topologically protected corner states. Using a generalized Stroh-Lie impedance formalism, the authors mapped orthotropic geometric frustration onto a four-band Dirac Hamiltonian and analytically proved that multiaxial compression triggers a cascade of higher-order topological transitions, from surface wrinkles to corner states.

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Higher-order topological insulators (HOTIs) extend the bulk-boundary correspondence to lower-dimensional features like hinges and corners. Prior mechanical HOTIs relied on discrete metamaterials or phononic lattices; this study shows that continuous hyperelastic materials under finite strain can intrinsically exhibit such phases via nonlinear geometric frustration.

## [Hierarchical Granular Metamaterials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22795) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers introduce hierarchical granular metamaterials that combine lightweight architected grains with crystal-inspired packings, demonstrating simultaneous increases in impact energy absorption per unit mass and reductions in transmitted peak force, validated by in situ nanomechanical experiments and nonlinear models. This work reconciles the trade-off between energy dissipation and lightweight design, outperforming conventional architected materials and paving the way for multifunctional metamaterials with programmable mechanical and transport properties. The metamaterials feature three design levels: lightweight architected grains with hollow elliptical inclusions, crystal-inspired grain packings, and functional gradients/defects within tessellations. Enhanced lateral grain expansion recruits neighboring grains, amplifying plastic and frictional dissipation across scales and constituent materials.

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Granular materials dissipate energy efficiently through intergranular interactions, but their disordered, dense nature limits control and lightweight integration. Architected materials offer tunable responses at low densities but tend to localize stress, reducing dissipation efficiency. This research combines both approaches in a hierarchical design to overcome these limitations.

## [Spontaneous magnon emission observed from hBN spin defects](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20996) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers report the first observation and control of spontaneous magnon emission from boron-vacancy centers in 2D hexagonal boron nitride (hBN), revealing a novel qubit-magnon dipole coupling channel that dominates at near-zero temperature. This discovery opens a new pathway for hybrid quantum systems, enabling quantum correlations via magnon emission in dense spin ensembles and advancing quantum technologies based on 2D materials. The spontaneous magnon emission is overshadowed by thermal magnons as temperature increases, showing a crossover from an emission-dominated cold reservoir to a thermally occupied spin bath. Theoretical modeling quantitatively captures the results, indicating potential for correlated spin dynamics.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 23, 04:00

**Background**: Hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) is a 2D material that hosts spin-active defects such as the negatively charged boron vacancy center (VB−). Magnons are quantized spin wave excitations in magnetic materials. Coupling between spin qubits and magnons is key for hybrid quantum systems, but spontaneous magnon emission had not been directly observed in this platform before.
