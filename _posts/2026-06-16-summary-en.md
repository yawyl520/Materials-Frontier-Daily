## [Precipitation strengthening is collective multi-dislocation phenomenon](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14890) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Large-scale atomistic simulations show that precipitation strengthening arises from collective multi-dislocation interactions, not just classical cutting or bowing mechanisms. This challenges a long-held dichotomy in physical metallurgy, providing a new mechanistic framework that could improve prediction and design of high-strength alloys. The simulations reveal concurrent dislocation accumulation at interfaces, storage within precipitates, and precipitate-mediated multiplication in the matrix, all contributing to strengthening as an emergent collective phenomenon.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Precipitation strengthening is a key method to improve strength and ductility in alloys by using nanoscale precipitates to hinder dislocation motion. Traditionally, dislocations were thought to either cut through or bow around precipitates. This study uses atomistic simulations to reveal a more complex, collective multi-dislocation behavior.

This work dramatically expands the geometric design space for flat-band materials, which are key for strongly correlated and topological phenomena. SkeleGen pins unconventional sublattice skeletons to symmetry-compatible Wyckoff positions while denoising the surrounding chemistry, yielding 9,352 stable flat-band candidates. ## [Unified Hybrid Functional Predicts Band Gaps Across Dimensions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15337) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A nonempirical screened-dielectric-dependent hybrid functional is introduced that accurately predicts fundamental band gaps for 100 materials spanning bulk, 2D, 1D, and mixed-dimensional systems, achieving near-GW accuracy at the computational cost of generalized Kohn-Sham theory. This work addresses a central challenge in electronic-structure theory by providing a unified framework for band gap prediction across all dimensionalities, enabling accurate material screening for semiconductors and insulators without the high cost of GW calculations. The key innovation is a geometry-independent dielectric response that captures intrinsic screening from bulk to low dimensions, self-consistently incorporated into the hybrid functional. Benchmarks on 100 materials show a screening-exchange-gap relation where reduced dimensionality weakens screening and strengthens nonlocal exchange, opening up band gaps.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Predicting band gaps accurately is crucial for designing semiconductors and insulators. Standard density functional theory (DFT) underestimates band gaps, while GW methods are accurate but computationally expensive. Hybrid functionals mix DFT exchange with a fraction of exact Hartree-Fock exchange; dielectric-dependent hybrid functionals tune this fraction based on the material's dielectric constant to improve accuracy. However, previous dielectric-dependent functionals were not suitable for low-dimensional materials where screening is anisotropic and geometry-dependent.

## [Confined Oxygen-Vacancy Migration Drives Ferroelectric Switching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16006) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new study reveals that robust ferroelectric switching in ultrathin Bi2SeO5 originates from confined oxygen-vacancy migration, not from conventional lattice distortions. This discovery challenges the conventional view that oxygen vacancies are detrimental to ferroelectricity, and establishes a new mechanism for ferroelectric switching in layered van der Waals oxides, potentially enabling new 2D ferroelectric devices with improved endurance. Oxygen vacancies preferentially form in SeO3 units and undergo reversible low-barrier rearrangements, generating a switchable out-of-plane polarization of ~16 μC cm⁻² at a vacancy concentration of 2.5%, while long-range diffusion is suppressed by high migration barriers.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Ferroelectric materials exhibit switchable spontaneous polarization and are used in non-volatile memories and sensors. Conventional ferroelectricity arises from ion displacements in the crystal lattice. Oxygen vacancies are usually mobile defects that cause leakage and degrade ferroelectric properties. The van der Waals oxide Bi2SeO5 was recently discovered to be ferroelectric despite its high symmetry, puzzling researchers.

## [Unified closure links fractals, Tsallis entropy, and memory in critical systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15750) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This paper derives a parameter-free algebraic relation α = D/(2D-1) that self-consistently links the fractal dimension D, Tsallis q-index, and memory exponent α in self-organized critical systems, eliminating the need for independent empirical fitting. This closure unifies three previously independent macroscopic features of critical systems and provides a universal theoretical framework validated across eight experimental systems, potentially transforming how fractality, nonextensive statistics, and non-Markovian dynamics are understood in complex systems. The relation α = D/(2D-1) together with q = 1+1/D (derived from Tsallis entropy extensivity on fractals) yields the known result α = 1/(3-q) as a consequence, not an assumption. The closure satisfies boundary conditions: α(1)=1 (ballistic transport in Euclidean space) and α→1/2 as D→∞ (maximally subdiffusive).

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Self-organized criticality (SOC) describes systems that naturally evolve to a critical state with power-law behavior. Fractal dimension quantifies the geometric complexity of such systems, Tsallis entropy generalizes Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy for nonextensive systems, and non-Markovian dynamics capture memory effects. Previously these three parameters were fitted independently; this work shows they are mathematically coupled through a simple self-consistency condition.

## [THz-TDS Detects Topological Surface Charge with High Precision](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15371) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers developed a new optical method using terahertz time-domain spectroscopy to detect topological surface charge accumulation in Cr-doped (Bi,Sb)2Te3 thin films, achieving sub-milliradian Faraday rotation precision. This technique is robust against longitudinal conductivity, unlike transport probes, making it applicable to a wider range of topological insulators. It could enable direct verification of the topological magnetoelectric effect and the four-dimensional quantum Hall effect. The method uses oblique-incidence THz-TDS to measure Faraday rotation with sub-milliradian precision, and the extracted charge accumulation η/Bz agrees at 45° and 60° incidence. For axion insulators, the predicted imaginary Faraday rotation is linear in frequency, with slope reflecting single-surface charge density.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Topological insulators have insulating interiors but conductive surface states protected by topology. The topological magnetoelectric effect (TME) predicts that an applied magnetic field induces quantized surface charge accumulation. Traditional transport measurements require ultralow longitudinal conductivity, limiting their applicability. Terahertz time-domain spectroscopy is an optical probe that can measure Faraday rotation, which is related to surface charge.

This study uses hybrid Monte Carlo molecular dynamics simulations to reveal that boron and carbon stabilize grain boundaries in Ni-Cr alloys through distinct thermodynamic pathways: carbon drives saturation-controlled stabilization by recruiting Cr, while boron lowers interfacial grand potential via localized ordering. These mechanistic insights provide a thermodynamic framework for grain boundary engineering in Ni-based superalloys, enabling design principles that leverage interstitial-substitutional interactions to improve high-temperature performance. The simulations quantify site-resolved segregation spectra and interfacial state variables, showing that carbon suppresses temperature-driven structural transformations while boron permits gradual structural evolution. The complementary pathways arise from coupled interactions between interstitial segregation and Cr redistribution.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 16, 04:00

**Background**: Grain boundary segregation by light interstitials like boron and carbon is critical for the performance of Ni-based superalloys used in high-temperature applications. Hybrid Monte Carlo molecular dynamics combines Monte Carlo sampling with molecular dynamics to efficiently explore the thermodynamic and structural evolution of interfaces. The interfacial grand potential is a key thermodynamic quantity that governs the stability of grain boundaries.
