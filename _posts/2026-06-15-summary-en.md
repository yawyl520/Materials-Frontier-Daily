## [Atom-by-Atom Mechanosynthesis: Donation and Abstraction on Si(100)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13876) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This work represents a significant step toward atom-by-atom fabrication, enabling precise manipulation of radical chemistry with three-dimensional positional control, which could revolutionize nanotechnology and molecular manufacturing. The study compares molecular tools designed for atomic donation versus abstraction, highlighting general principles for selective and reliable mechanosynthetic functionality on atomically clean crystalline Si(100).

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: Mechanosynthesis uses mechanical constraints to direct chemical reactions at the atomic scale, enabling positional control of covalent bond formation. Conventional scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) can image and manipulate individual atoms, but reproducible manipulation of covalently bonded atoms has been a longstanding challenge. Inverted-mode STM (IM-STM) overcomes this by controlling both the sample and probe atomic configurations, as demonstrated in prior work.

## [MnAu2: A Room-Temperature p-Wave Altermagnet with Hedgehog Berry Curvature](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14246) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers demonstrate that helimagnetic MnAu2 exhibits metallic p-wave magnetism and hedgehog-type Berry curvature at room temperature, confirmed by first-principles calculations and band-unfolding analysis. This discovery provides a rare room-temperature metallic p-wave magnet, advancing altermagnetism and topological spintronics by enabling spin-split bands and Berry-curvature-driven Hall effects without external fields. The p-wave spin texture appears as a single electron pocket at the M̃-point, and the helical spin ordering induces hedgehog Berry curvature linked to nonlinear Hall and spin Hall effects, with experimental Curie temperature TC=335–370 K.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: Altermagnets are a class of collinear compensated magnets with even-parity spin texture in momentum space, distinct from ferromagnets and antiferromagnets. p-Wave magnets exhibit odd-parity spin splitting due to noncollinear spin ordering. Berry curvature is a geometric property of electronic bands that governs topological transport phenomena such as the anomalous Hall effect.

## [New framework predicts internal conversion rates in spin defects](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14545) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers propose a first-principles framework that calculates internal conversion rates in optically active spin defects using multi-configurational TDDFT and analytical non-adiabatic couplings, achieving quantitative agreement with experiment for the NV- center in diamond and discovering a new non-radiative channel in SiC. This work resolves order-of-magnitude discrepancies between previous theoretical approximations and experimental measurements of internal conversion rates, which is critical for designing spin defects for quantum technologies such as quantum sensing and computing. The framework incorporates multi-configurational effects via many-body wavefunctions in time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) and includes all phonon modes through analytical non-adiabatic couplings. The method achieves quantitative agreement for the NV- center in diamond and identifies a previously overlooked non-radiative channel in the divacancy triplet lifetime in 4H-SiC.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: Optically active spin defects in solids, such as the nitrogen-vacancy (NV) center in diamond, are promising for quantum technologies, but their performance depends on non-radiative decay processes like internal conversion. Internal conversion is a decay process where an excited state returns to the ground state by emitting phonons (lattice vibrations) rather than photons. Previous computational methods often underestimated internal conversion rates by orders of magnitude, hindering the design of high-performance quantum materials.

## [Epstein zeta method cuts many-body lattice sum time from weeks to minutes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.11989) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new method based on Epstein zeta functions enables efficient computation of many-body lattice sums, reducing runtime from weeks to minutes for three-body interactions, and generalizing to n-body sums with only linear increase in cost. This breakthrough solves a long-standing computational bottleneck in condensed matter physics and materials science, enabling accurate study of many-body interactions that were previously prohibitively expensive. It will accelerate research on material stability and properties influenced by three-body and higher-order forces. The method expresses many-body lattice sums as singular integrals over products of Epstein zeta functions. For three-dimensional three-body ATM lattice sums, the runtime drops from weeks to minutes, and the approach achieves full precision for exponents greater than the system dimension.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: Many-body interactions, such as the Axilrod-Teller-Muto (ATM) potential for three-body forces, are important for accurate material property predictions but are computationally demanding due to lattice sums that scale poorly with number of particles and body order. The Epstein zeta function generalizes the Riemann zeta function to higher-dimensional lattice sums and is a key mathematical tool for evaluating such sums analytically or semi-analytically, reducing computational cost.

Using simulations, the paper demonstrates that chirality (self-spinning) induces a 'thinning-by-spinning' effect in dense chiral fluids, where viscosity decreases with increasing spinning rate. A Green-Kubo relation with a chirality-dependent effective temperature quantitatively describes the viscosity, and a handedness asymmetry emerges at strong shear. This work provides a unified framework for understanding the rheology of dense chiral suspensions, which are common in active matter and soft materials. The findings could impact the design of novel fluids with controllable viscosity and flow properties. The study uses a 2D Lennard-Jones model with transverse interactions to simulate dense chiral fluids. In the liquid regime, the viscosity is described by a Green-Kubo relation using a chirality-dependent effective temperature, while at strong forcing, a handedness asymmetry leads to stress relaxation via string-like flow channels.

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: Chiral fluids are composed of self-spinning particles, breaking mirror symmetry and giving rise to odd transport coefficients like odd viscosity. In soft matter, such fluids exhibit unique phenomena such as dissipationless flow. The Green-Kubo relation relates transport coefficients to equilibrium fluctuations, and effective temperatures are used to describe nonequilibrium active systems.

## [Conserved active systems have nonlocal stationary measure](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14483) ⭐️ 9.0/10

This paper demonstrates that the stationary measure in conserved active systems is generically nonlocal, precluding the standard Landau-Ginzburg expansion. The result is derived perturbatively deep in the disordered phase for models including Active Model B+, the Nonreciprocal Cahn-Hilliard model, and the Toner-Tu model. This finding has broad implications for understanding non-equilibrium systems, as it connects several important active matter models and shows that standard equilibrium statistical mechanics approaches fail for conserved active field theories. It challenges the use of free-energy-based descriptions in such systems. The nonlocality is linked to a dynamical conservation law and is demonstrated by expanding in nonlinearity about a Gaussian-model limit. The analysis covers models with conservation laws such as Active Model A, Active Model B+, Model AB, the Nonreciprocal Cahn-Hilliard model, and the Toner-Tu model.

rss · arXiv Soft Condensed Matter · Jun 15, 04:00

**Background**: In equilibrium systems with detailed balance, the stationary measure is local, meaning distant spatial domains are statistically independent. Active systems break detailed balance and can exhibit nonlocal stationary measure even for local dynamics. The Landau-Ginzburg expansion is a common tool to construct free-energy functionals for equilibrium systems. This paper shows that such an expansion is generically impossible for conserved active field theories.
