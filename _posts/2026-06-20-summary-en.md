## [Direct imaging reveals long-lived bulk photovoltaic effect in BiFeO3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01729) ⭐️ 10.0/10

Researchers used contactless pump-probe microscopy to directly image spatiotemporal carrier dynamics in single-crystal BiFeO3, observing asymmetric transport that persists for nanoseconds. This long-lived effect is attributed to asymmetric momentum scattering by oxygen vacancies, contrasting with conventional short-lived shift or phonon ballistic current mechanisms. This direct observation challenges established theories of the bulk photovoltaic effect and provides a new microscopic mechanism—defect-assisted asymmetric scattering—that can be exploited to design more efficient photovoltaic and optoelectronic devices. It also opens avenues for controlling symmetry- and defect-driven photoresponses in ferroelectric and quantum materials. The experiments employed contactless pump-probe microscopy on single-domain BiFeO3 single crystals, achieving spatiotemporal resolution of carrier motion. Monte Carlo simulations indicate that oxygen vacancies are the key defects causing asymmetric momentum scattering, which sustains the photocurrent for several nanoseconds.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: The bulk photovoltaic effect (BPVE) occurs in non-centrosymmetric materials and produces a photovoltage beyond the bandgap limit. Conventional mechanisms include the shift current, arising from wavefunction displacement during photoexcitation, and the phonon ballistic current, driven by asymmetric carrier-phonon scattering. Both are typically short-lived (sub-picosecond), making the nanosecond-scale transport observed here surprising.

## [Hartree-Fock Limit Achieved for Solids in LAPW](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20116) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new method enables reaching the Hartree-Fock limit for molecules and solids within the LAPW framework, achieving total energy precision of a few μHa. The study provides reference data and benchmarks for 14 semiconductors and insulators. This breakthrough removes a fundamental limitation of LAPW for nonlocal exchange, enabling highly precise reference energies for benchmarking other electronic structure methods. It improves error control in hybrid-functional calculations and paves the way for X-ray spectroscopy simulations directly from hybrid-functional core orbitals. The method constructs radial basis functions and core orbitals consistently with the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian, overcoming previous LAPW limitations. The standard semi-local approach remains accurate for practical relative energies, but the new method provides all-electron benchmarks for basis-set and pseudopotential assessment.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: The linearized augmented plane wave (LAPW) method is an all-electron full-potential approach in density functional theory (DFT) known for high precision. However, it struggled with nonlocal exchange, such as that in Hartree-Fock theory, due to inconsistent construction of basis functions. This work resolves that inconsistency.

## [Breaking symmetry yields tenfold better microwave varactor dielectric](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20371) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers synthesized Ruddlesden-Popper thin films with broken out-of-plane crystallographic symmetry, enabling a low-loss, out-of-plane tunable dielectric for parallel-plate varactors. The best film, (Ba0.45Sr0.55TiO3)8(Ba0.45Sr0.55)O, achieves a tenfold improvement in figure of merit at 10 GHz compared to conventional designs. This breakthrough overcomes a key limitation of Ruddlesden-Popper dielectrics—their in-plane device structure—by enabling the compact, efficient parallel-plate varactor design. The tenfold improvement in figure of merit could significantly advance tunable monolithic microwave integrated circuits for communications and radar. The material is derived from Ba_xSr_1-xTiO3 (BST) but assembled into a Ruddlesden-Popper phase with formula (ATiO3)_nAO, where A = Ba0.45Sr0.55 and n = 8. The out-of-plane tunability was achieved by breaking the crystallographic symmetry that previously forced in-plane operation.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: Varactors (voltage-tunable capacitors) are essential for microwave circuits. Ruddlesden-Popper (RP) phases are layered perovskite structures with very low dielectric loss, but their inherent symmetry had limited devices to an in-plane electrode configuration, which is less efficient. Parallel-plate varactors offer smaller size and higher electric fields, but require out-of-plane tunable dielectrics, which were previously challenging for RP phases.

## [Efficient Quantum Circuit Decomposition of Tangent-Fermion Dirac Operator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19020) ⭐️ 9.0/10

The authors demonstrate that the tangent-fermion discretization of the Dirac operator allows a linear-combination-of-unitaries (LCU) representation with a term count that is independent of lattice size and a subnormalization factor of order unity, enabling efficient quantum simulation without fermion doubling. This work removes a long-standing obstruction in quantum simulation of relativistic fermions by solving the fermion doubling problem without sacrificing locality or efficiency, which is crucial for quantum algorithms in high-energy physics and lattice gauge theory. The Dirac equation is reformulated as a generalized eigenvalue problem with a local operator pencil, where each member has an exact LCU with constant term count and subnormalization factor of order unity, on par with elliptic operators. This provides an efficient block-encoding primitive for Dirac spectra and Green functions.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: Fermion doubling is a lattice artifact that introduces spurious fermion species, and traditional discretizations either break locality or symmetry to avoid it. Tangent-fermion discretization uses a tangent function to avoid doubling but was previously non-local, leading to inefficient quantum circuits. The linear combination of unitaries (LCU) is a quantum algorithmic primitive that decomposes operators into weighted sums of unitaries, enabling non-unitary operations on quantum computers.

Non-Abelian anyons, like Fibonacci anyons, are promising for topological quantum computation due to their braiding properties. This paper combines these concepts to propose a 3D foliated system hosting non-Abelian Fibonacci anyons.

## [Nuclear spin phase locking via spin-orbit coupling in silicon](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20340) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have demonstrated phase-locking of a 29Si nuclear spin ensemble in a silicon quantum dot using only the internal electronic spin-orbit coupling as a phase reference, enabling coherent precession measurement and inhomogeneous dephasing characterization without external phase references. This method eliminates the need for external phase references, such as microwave fields, for coherent nuclear spin control, simplifying solid-state quantum information processing. It opens new routes for harnessing the long coherence times of nuclear spin ensembles in quantum computing. The phase-locking is achieved by driving the nuclear spins with quantum-dot electrons, causing them to align to a phase determined by the electronic spin-orbit coupling and the timing of the drive protocol. The results are corroborated by detailed many-body numerical simulations of the electron-nuclear system.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: Nuclear spins in solids have extremely long coherence times, making them attractive for quantum information storage and processing. However, coherent control typically requires external phase references like microwave fields. Silicon quantum dots provide a platform where electron spins can be manipulated, and the hyperfine interaction couples electrons to nearby nuclear spins. This work leverages the intrinsic spin-orbit coupling in the quantum dot as an internal phase reference, avoiding external controls.

## [HEACalculator: Open-Source Python Tool for HEA Thermodynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19661) ⭐️ 8.0/10

HEACalculator is an open-source Python package that computes 16 thermodynamic descriptors and evaluates 8 solid-solution formation rules for high-entropy alloys (HEAs). It provides CLI, GUI, and API access modes, consolidating commonly used calculations into a single standardized tool. This tool addresses a key bottleneck in HEA design by providing a unified, accessible resource for thermodynamic calculations, which will accelerate alloy screening and reduce duplication of effort. It standardizes the application of widely used descriptors and rules, improving reproducibility and efficiency in the research community. The package calculates descriptors including mixing enthalpy, configurational entropy, valence electron concentration, atomic size mismatch, and stability parameters like Omega, Lambda, and Phi. It evaluates eight published prediction criteria, such as the Hume-Rothery rules and VEC-based phase stability.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 19, 04:00

**Background**: High-entropy alloys (HEAs) are multi-principal-element alloys that often form simple solid solutions rather than complex intermetallics. A critical early step in HEA design is predicting whether a given composition will form a single-phase solid solution, which traditionally relies on calculating thermodynamic descriptors and applying empirical rules. HEACalculator automates this process using a curated dataset and multiple access interfaces.
