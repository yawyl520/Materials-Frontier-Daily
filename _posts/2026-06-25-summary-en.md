## [Quantum-enabled active matter at atomic scale demonstrated](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24615) ŌŁÉ’ĖÅ 10.0/10

Researchers experimentally demonstrated quantum-enabled active matter at the atomic scale by using spin exchange between Cs-133 and Rb-87 atoms to convert spin energy into directed motion. This work bridges active matter and quantum physics, opening new avenues for studying non-equilibrium thermodynamics at the fundamental size limit. It could lead to novel quantum materials and nanoscale devices powered by quantum spin interactions. The experiment confined Cs-133 atoms in an optical dipole trap within an ultracold Rb-87 bath. The observed dynamics were quantitatively reproduced using a parameter-free active Langevin model and event-driven Monte Carlo simulations, confirming that quantum spin exchange is the microscopic origin of activity.

**Background**: Active matter consists of particles that extract energy from their environment to generate motion, typically studied in biological or colloidal systems. At atomic scales, quantum effects become dominant, and spin exchange is a quantum mechanical process where two atoms exchange their spin states, conserving total angular momentum but allowing kinetic energy changes. This experiment realizes active motion at the ultimate single-atom limit.
