Weyl semimetals host linear band crossings (Weyl points) and can exhibit extreme electronic asymmetry when particle-hole symmetry is broken, leading to strong helicity selectivity.

Berry curvature in parameter space can have monopole singularities; here strain-space Berry curvature monopole refers to such a feature in the space of lattice deformations.

## [Giant Peltier effect in ferroelectric insulator via bound charges](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25767) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers report a giant Peltier effect in a ferroelectric insulator, with a coefficient exceeding 100 V near the phase transition, attributed to bound charges rather than mobile charges. This was measured using multi-harmonic lock-in thermography under an AC electric field. This discovery challenges the conventional wisdom that thermoelectric effects require mobile charge carriers, opening a new paradigm for thermal management using dielectric materials. It could lead to highly efficient solid-state heat pumps or coolers leveraging the giant Peltier coefficient. The Peltier coefficient exceeds 100 V, which is several orders of magnitude larger than typical values in conductors. The effect arises from field-induced displacement current and bound charge dynamics in a ferroelectric near its paraelectric transition.

rss · arXiv Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics · Jun 26, 04:00

**Background**: Thermoelectric effects, such as the Seebeck and Peltier effects, traditionally involve the movement of mobile charge carriers (electrons or holes) in conductors to convert heat and electricity. Ferroelectrics are insulating materials with spontaneous electric polarization that can be switched by an electric field; near their phase transition, bound charges contribute to novel phenomena. Multi-harmonic lock-in thermography is a sensitive technique that measures temperature changes by analyzing harmonic components of the thermal response to an AC field.

## [Nanoscale Phase Distribution Controls Exchange Bias in Ni-Cr/NiO Nanoparticles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26330) ⭐️ 8.0/10

This study reveals that exchange bias in Ni-Cr/NiO nanoparticles is governed by the nanoscale spatial distribution and interfacial topology of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic phases, not just their presence. At 10 K, a significant negative exchange bias of 0.8 kOe and coercivity enhancement of 1.4 kOe were observed. This work provides mechanistic insights into exchange bias in complex nanoscale heterostructures, which is crucial for designing next-generation spintronic devices and magnetic memory systems. The predictive framework linking DFT calculations to spin dynamics simulations enables engineering of exchange-coupled nanomagnets that operate beyond the superparamagnetic limit. Using Ni-Cr/NiO nanoparticles as a model system, the study combined gas-phase synthesis, advanced magnetic characterization, and first-principles-informed spin-dynamics simulations. At low Cr content, Cr segregation reduced exchange bias; at high Cr content, Cr accumulation inside the core caused similar reduction, leading to a temperature-driven inversion from negative to positive exchange bias and a crossover from exchange-dominated to dipolar interactions.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 26, 04:00

**Background**: Exchange bias is a phenomenon observed in ferromagnet-antiferromagnet interfaces, widely used in magnetic memory and spintronics. In nanoscale multiphase particles, the spatial distribution of phases can significantly alter magnetic properties. Gas-phase synthesis, such as magnetron-sputtering inert-gas condensation, allows precise control over nanoparticle composition and structure. First-principles spin-dynamics simulations combine density functional theory with time evolution of magnetic moments to predict behavior.

This method directly addresses a critical bottleneck in atomic layer deposition (ALD) process development and other fields requiring costly measurements, enabling accurate curve inference with as few as seven measurements. The framework benchmarks on five kinetically distinct isotherm families, achieving noise-floor accuracy within a 20-measurement budget and guaranteeing no non-monotone posterior draws, with predicted pulse times erring only conservatively near saturation.

rss · arXiv Materials Science · Jun 26, 04:00

**Background**: Self-limiting saturation curves describe monotonic responses that rise from zero to a plateau, common in gas adsorption, enzyme kinetics, and atomic layer deposition. Standard Gaussian process surrogates can produce unphysical dips on sparse data. I-splines are monotone spline bases that enforce non-decreasing shape constraints, and Bayesian frameworks allow active learning via uncertainty sampling to choose the next measurement efficiently.
